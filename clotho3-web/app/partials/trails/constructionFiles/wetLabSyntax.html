Wet labs often use human-readable construction files to succinctly describe a set of reactions.

These construction files often refer to Parts, or DNA sequences with an encapsulated function, by name.

For example, pPROBE_GFP is the gene we want to clone, and pUC19 is a plasmid backbone, and referred to by name in our GFP cloning.

PCR primer1/primer2 on pPROBE_GFP (pcr_product)
Digest pcr_product (EcoRI/HindIII, fragments)
GelPurify fragments (L, probeDigested)
Digest pUC19 (EcoRI/HindIII, fragments2)
GelPurify fragments2 (pUC19Digested)
Ligate probeDigested/pUC19Digested (final)
